= Pillar 2 - Cost Optimization

== What is it about?

Cost Optimization in WAF++ means efficiently managing the costs of infrastructure, applications, and services without compromising quality or security. The goal is to create transparency, avoid unnecessary spending, and direct investments where they deliver the greatest value.

== What is being done?

* *Cost analysis & monitoring:* Using tools for cost monitoring and forecasting.
* *Resource optimization:* Shutting down unused resources, rightsizing instances.
* *Automation:* Scaling on demand to avoid over-provisioning.
* *Budgeting & forecasting:* Planning and controlling budgets for projects and teams.
* *Using reservations & discounts:* Leveraging reserved instances, spot instances, and license optimization.

== What needs to be considered?

* *Transparency:* Clear allocation of costs to projects and owners.
* *Governance:* Policies for spending and approval processes.
* *Trade-offs:* Cost optimization must not come at the expense of performance or security.
* *Regular reviews:* Cost structures must be continuously reviewed and adjusted.

== Where is this headed?

Long-term, Cost Optimization as part of WAF++ aims to achieve the following goals:

* *FinOps culture:* Embedding cost awareness in all teams.
* *Automated cost control:* Dynamic adjustment of resources based on usage patterns.
* *Predictive analytics:* Using AI to forecast costs and optimization potential.
* *Cost as a KPI:* Cost optimization becomes a strategic factor in architectural decisions.
